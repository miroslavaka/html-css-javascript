Ada Lovelace

English mathematician

Timeline of Ada's life :

  • 1815 - born in London, England
  • at the age of 8 - experienced headaches that obscured her vision
  • 1829 - paralyzed after a bout of measles
  • by 1931 - able to walk with crutches, developed her mathematical and technological skills
  • from 1932 - her mathematical abilities began to emerge, and her interest in mathematics dominated the majority of her adult life
  • 1835 - married William, 8th Baron King
  • 1838 - became the Countess of Lovelace
  • 1842-43 - translated the Italian mathematician Luigi Menabrea's article on Babbage's newest proposed machine, the Analytical Engine. With the article, she appended a set of notes. The notes are around three times longer than the article itself and include complete detail, a method for calculating a sequence of Bernoulli numbers using the Analytical Engine
  • 1844 - commented to a friend Woronzow Greig about her desire to create a mathematical model for how the brain gives rise to thoughts and nerves to feelings
  • 1851 - ambitious attempt to create a mathematical model
  • 1852 - died from cancer

Lovelace believed that intuition and imagination were critical to effectively applying mathematical and scientific concepts. She valued metaphysics as much as mathematics, viewing both as tools for exploring "the unseen worlds around us"

More information about Ada's life and work is in her Wikipedia article.
